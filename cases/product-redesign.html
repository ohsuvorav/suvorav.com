Case studies
Threecolts · 2023–2025

How I redesigned the whole product. Almost solo.

Context

When I joined Threecolts I took ownership of an acquired product — built by its founder over 10 years. Technically solid. But activation was broken. The customers who got through the setup loved it and stayed for years. The problem was almost no one got there.

Problem

So I decided to focus on decreasing time-to-value and showcasing the aha-moment ASAP. However, our GTM was SLG, and let's say no one saw it as a priority.

What I did
Part 1 - Setup moment

To keep it short: I reverse-engineered successfully retained customers. And I started working onwards: setup -> aha moment -> activated (engagement threshold).

First I revamped Email setup. Talking about its screen UX issues is a rabbit hole.

It felt like an admin panel for the developer who built the feature. While the new design wasn't sexy, the interface perfectly answered all setup questions and the number of calls I joined dropped from five per week to zero.

Part 2 - Aha moment

That time I defined it as when the system starts doing the job instead the user (before AI has become a thing). So the goal has become to make the invisible visible.

So I built "object activity log" that surfaced every action the system took per object: assignments, status changes, email threads, rule triggers — all in one place.

Previously to understand what's happening users would need to learn 4 separate things: trigger's log, email's log, triggers setup and messages itself.

Before
After

When existing customers' saw it for the first time: "Wait, it does all that?" That awareness triggered the next question: "What else can it do?" Feature exploration jumped. Adoption followed.

Despite the fact we consolidated everything in one place natively, we also got rid of the need of 2 popups: mailing log, change log.

Impact

Despite the fact it's become cleaner and customers wrote in caps lock how much they "LOOVE it", UI has been significantly constrained by given design system.

Nevertheless I learned how to design and build design systems. Look what kind of design PRDs I've been creating and I had dozens of such files tinkering.

Couple of disclaimers:

  • I didn't know how to design
  • We had no design system to start from
  • I worked with 3 designers and fired 1, because they didn't meet the standards. 1.5 year later the wonderful lady joined my team and taught me about design system nuances and design processes. Along with her we redesigned additionally Triggers and Analytics pages.
  • I'm proud how I shipped "tabs" for the object view earlier than Slack
  • Our main competitor rolled out the similar design after two years, which is pure global market validation of my work. Yet it's worth mentioning: there are ~ 10 PMs + 10 designers required to reach the same quality bar.

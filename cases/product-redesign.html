Case studies
Threecolts · 2023–2025

Redesigned the whole product. Solo.

Once I stabilized baseline in product and biz ops and my team reached initial Product-Market Fit. I focused on compounding its growth. Product was great, but very few customers were reaching its aha moment quickly. The goal was make it obvious from the first glance like in Fin (prev. Intercom) and Linear.

Problem

Aha moment

Earlier I reverse-engineered successfully retained customers: engagement treshold (N tickets / mo per support agent) -> aha moment (order details on the right powered by ChannelReply) -> setup (email / contact form setup). Since I already streamlined setup part, and onboarding with our account managers I focused on showcasing aha-moment: send message seeing order details on the right.

I got inspired by this Intercom worfklow https://mobbin.com/flows/3c3cc987-55ef-4c59-b71b-a41555bbf308?tab=screens&scrollToScreenIndex=0

Helpdesk setup: to send a message you need to connect your email, ideally move from competitor alternative with your ops. It requires hadn-holding. I initially compensated it with Account Managers and Help center, now I wanted to make it self-serve

So we needed to show depth without overwhelming users

What I did

Three moves up the ladder.

Move 1 — Make the invisible visible

The biggest activation blocker wasn't missing features. It was that no one knew what the product was already doing.

First thing I built "object activity log" that surfaced every action the system took per object: assignments, status changes, email threads, rule triggers — all visible in one place.

Previously to understand what's happening users would need to learn 4 separate things: trigger's log, email's log, triggers setup and messages itself

Even existing customers' were puzzled when they saw it: "Wait, it does all that?" That awareness triggered the next question: "What else can it do?" Feature exploration jumped. Adoption followed.

Move 2 — Cut the steps

Redesigned the core inbox and workflow flows end-to-end. The before: a dense list with no visual hierarchy, everything requiring an open to understand. The after: clear grouping, faster triage, context visible without clicking into every ticket.

Move 3 — Email setup: prototype first, PRD second

One setup path was blocking everyone: email configuration. I was receiving setup questions daily despite a step-by-step help doc. I rebuilt the flow from scratch.

The process: opened Figma, built a low-fidelity clickable prototype, validated it with the support team before writing a single line of spec. Then I rewrote every tooltip using the exact language customers used when they were confused — pulled directly from support call recordings. Not generic help text. The words they already had.

Setup support tickets dropped to zero.

Impact

Onboarding dropped from 8 weeks to 1.

Setup support tickets about email setup dropped to zero.

Feature adoption increased.

Activation got streamlined

Our main competitor needed two years and a full team of PMs and designers to reach this quality bar. We matched it with one PM, no design system to start from, and first-principles thinking from a blank Figma file.
